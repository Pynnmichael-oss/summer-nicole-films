Hi, I'm Summer.

The story

I'm a Texas-based photographer and filmmaker, and I've been documenting love stories for going on seven years. I got into this work because I couldn't stop thinking about how quickly the feeling of a day fades. The dress, the venue — those you remember. But the look on his face when he saw you? The way your mom squeezed your hand right before you walked in? Those need a little help.

My style is documentary. I stay quiet, I stay close, and I wait for things to unfold naturally rather than engineering moments that look good on paper but feel hollow in real life. I want your photos and films to feel like memory — not a highlight reel. The real stuff. The ordinary beautiful things that are happening all around you while you're too busy living to notice.

I fell in love with film photography in college and never really came back. There's something about the warmth of it — the grain, the color — that digital has always been chasing. I bring both to every wedding, because some moments deserve to live in both worlds.

I'm based in Texas and I travel a lot. Hill Country weddings, Nashville elopements, Denver mountain ceremonies — I'm in. If your venue is beautiful and your love is real, I want to be there.

I don't just document weddings. I preserve the feeling of them.

How I work

Quiet presence.
Real moments.

On your wedding day I'll be the person you forget is there — until you see the photos. I move light, I stay out of the way, and I never stop watching. The dad fixing his son's collar before the ceremony. The bridesmaids laughing at something only they know. The moment right before you walk through those doors.

I shoot with a second shooter for most weddings so nothing gets missed. I arrive early and stay late. I care about the whole day, not just the ceremony.
