The approach

How a film gets made.

A wedding film isn't a highlight reel. It's a document. I capture the ambient sound — the laughs, the vows, the clinking glasses — alongside the visuals, because that's what puts you back in the room when you watch it five years from now.

I edit every film myself. No outsourcing. I spend hours in the footage until I find the sequence that feels true to how the day actually moved — the quiet before, the joy during, the exhale after. Then I find the music that holds it all together.

Most wedding films run 5–8 minutes. Some days earn 12. I'll always err on the side of keeping more in rather than cutting for the sake of brevity. It's your day — you deserve all of it.
